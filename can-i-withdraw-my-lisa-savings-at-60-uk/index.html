Can I withdraw my LISA savings at 60 in the UK?

Learn when and how to withdraw your Lifetime ISA (LISA) savings at State Pension age, including tax implications and expert advice.

What is a Lifetime ISA?

A Lifetime ISA (LISA) is a type of savings account designed specifically for first-time homebuyers or those aged 18-39. It's a government-backed scheme that allows you to save up to £4,000 per year, with the government adding a 25% bonus on top of your contributions. This means if you contribute £1,000, the government will add an extra £250, making it a total of £1,250.

How do I withdraw my LISA savings at 60 in the UK?

If you're aged 60 or above, you can withdraw your LISA funds, but there are specific rules to follow. You'll need to use the money for one of the two approved purposes: buying a first home or retirement. If you withdraw for a reason other than these two, you'll face an income tax charge and may impact your entitlement to means-tested benefits.

To withdraw your LISA savings, you'll need to contact your provider and request a withdrawal. You can do this online, over the phone, or by visiting a branch in person. Be aware that withdrawing from a LISA will trigger an income tax charge, which is calculated based on your withdrawal amount. For example, if you withdraw £10,000, you'll be charged 25% income tax, leaving you with £7,500.

Implications for pension planning at State Pension age

When considering withdrawing from a LISA at or above State Pension age, it's essential to understand the long-term implications on your overall pension planning. While accessing your LISA funds can provide a welcome injection of cash, it may also impact your retirement savings and pension pot.

To maintain a comfortable retirement, consider the following:

* Your retirement income goals * The size of your pension pot * Any potential tax implications

By understanding these factors, you can make informed decisions about your LISA withdrawals and plan for a secure financial future.

Consequences of withdrawing from a LISA at or above 60

While accessing your LISA funds may seem appealing, it's crucial to consider the potential drawbacks. Withdrawing from a LISA will trigger an income tax charge, which can reduce your overall take-home amount. Additionally, you may impact your entitlement to means-tested benefits.

Here are some key things to keep in mind:

* Income tax charges: 25% of withdrawal for those aged 60-74 and full income tax for those over 74 * Means-tested benefits impact: Reduced eligibility or potentially lost
