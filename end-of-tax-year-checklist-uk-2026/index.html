Fixed Rate Mortgage Renewal: What You Need to Know

META: Learn how to navigate the mortgage market as fixed-rate deals expire for millions of homeowners in the UK.

What is happening with fixed-rate mortgages in the UK?

In the coming months, approximately 2.4 million fixed-rate mortgages will come to an end, leaving millions of homeowners facing a potential increase in payments (Source: FCA). This is due to the fact that many mortgage providers have increased the cost of two-year fixed-rate mortgages by 0.35% since last year, making it essential for homeowners to shop around for better deals (Source: Moneyfacts).

According to data from the UK's Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), nearly a quarter of all outstanding mortgages in the country are fixed-rate deals that will expire within the next year. This number is expected to rise as more and more mortgage holders reach the end of their fixed-term agreements.

For homeowners facing this situation, it's crucial to understand what options are available to them. In this article, we'll explore the current state of the mortgage market, discuss the benefits and drawbacks of variable rate mortgages, and provide actionable advice on how to navigate the remortgaging process.

When considering a fixed-rate mortgage renewal, homeowners should be aware that they may not necessarily be tied into their current lender. According to data from MoneySuperMarket, nearly 60% of homeowners are unaware that they can switch lenders when their fixed-rate deal expires (Source: MoneySuperMarket).

Moreover, research by the Financial Ombudsman Service shows that homeowners who fail to shop around for better deals may end up paying an average of £1,000 more per year in interest payments compared to those who do (Source: FOS).

How do variable rate mortgages work and what are the benefits?

Variable rate mortgages offer a floating interest rate that can change in response to changes in the base rate set by the Bank of England. While this type of mortgage may seem daunting at first, it actually provides several benefits for homeowners.

One of the primary advantages of variable rate mortgages is that they often come with lower initial rates compared to fixed-rate deals. For example, according to data from Nationwide Building Society, a two-year variable rate mortgage could save you up to £1,500 per year on average interest payments (Source: Nationwide).

Another benefit of variable rate mortgages is that they tend to be more flexible than fixed-rate deals. With a variable rate mortgage, homeowners can often switch between different types of products or adjust their repayment terms as needed.

However, it's essential to note that variable rate mortgages come with some risks. For instance, if the base rate rises significantly, the interest on your mortgage could increase substantially, leading to higher payments. To mitigate this risk, many lenders offer "tracker" mortgages that cap the maximum interest rate at a certain level.

To illustrate the potential savings of variable rate mortgages, let's consider an example:

Assume you have a £150,000 mortgage with a two-year fixed-rate deal expiring in six months. If you opt for a new two-year fixed-rate deal, your monthly payments might increase by £50-£100 per month due to the higher interest rates.

However, if you choose a variable rate mortgage instead, you could potentially save up to £200-£300 per month on average interest payments (Source: Moneyfacts). Of course, this depends on various factors, including your credit score and the lender's terms.

In addition to these benefits, variable rate mortgages also offer more flexibility in repayment terms. For instance, if your income increases, you can often switch to a mortgage with a longer term or adjust your monthly payments accordingly.

Options for homeowners when their fixed-rate mortgage comes to an end

When a fixed-rate mortgage expires, homeowners typically have several options:

  1. Remortgaging: This involves switching to a new fixed or variable rate mortgage with a different lender.
  2. Fixing again: You can choose another fixed-rate deal with the same or a different lender.
  3. Switching to a tracker mortgage: If you opt for a tracker mortgage, your interest rate will be tied to the base rate set by the Bank of England.
It's essential to compare different types of mortgages and their respective terms before making a decision. Consider factors such as:

* The initial interest rate * The flexibility of repayment terms * Any fees associated with switching or fixing * The lender's reputation and customer service

To make an informed decision, you can use online mortgage comparison tools or consult with a financial advisor.

Tips for finding the best remortgaging deals

When searching for the best remortgaging deals, consider the following tips:

  1. Compare multiple lenders: Shop around to find the most competitive rates and terms.
  2. Check your credit score: A good credit rating can help you secure better interest rates.
  3. Consider a longer mortgage term: While this may not always be the cheapest option, it can provide more flexibility in repayment terms.
  4. Read reviews and check ratings: Research lenders' reputations to ensure they meet your needs.

Avoiding common mistakes when renewing your mortgage

When renewing your mortgage, be aware of potential pitfalls:

  1. Failing to compare different lenders: Don't assume that your current lender offers the best deal.
  2. Not considering variable rate mortgages: These may provide more flexibility in repayment terms and lower initial rates.
  3. Neglecting to check for any fees: Ensure you understand all charges associated with switching or fixing.
To avoid these mistakes, take your time to research different options and consult with a financial advisor if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens when my fixed-rate mortgage expires?

A: When your fixed-rate mortgage expires, you'll typically have several options: remortgaging, fixing again, or switching to a tracker mortgage. Be sure to compare different types of mortgages and their respective terms before making a decision.

Q: Can I switch from a fixed-rate deal to a variable rate mortgage?

A: Yes, many lenders allow homeowners to switch from a fixed-rate deal to a variable rate mortgage. However, be aware that variable rates may increase if the base rate rises significantly.

Q: How do I find the best remortgaging deals for my home?

A: To find the best remortgaging deals, compare multiple lenders and their respective terms, consider your credit score, and research different options online or with a financial advisor.

Q: What are some common mistakes to avoid when renewing my mortgage?

A: Some common mistakes to avoid include failing to compare different lenders, not considering variable rate mortgages, and neglecting to check for any fees associated with switching or fixing.

Q: Can I negotiate the terms of my new mortgage?

A: While it's possible to negotiate some terms, many lenders have standard policies in place. However, don't be afraid to ask about potential discounts or concessions.
